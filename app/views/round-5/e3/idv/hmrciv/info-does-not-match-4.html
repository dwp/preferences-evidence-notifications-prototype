The information you gave does not match our records

This means that some or all of the details you entered were incorrect.

You cannot use documents containing out of date information to confirm your identity. If you have changed your personal details, like your name for example, you must make sure your documents and records with HMRC and other organisations are up to date.

More detailed help and advice on confirming your identity is available below.

Payslip

You need a payslip from within the last 3 months to use this option. You cannot use a payslip that has a zero or negative (refund) amount.

You will need to tell us:

  • the amount of National Insurance
  • the amount of tax deducted

Get these figures from the ‘deductions’ section, and do not use the ‘year to date’ figures. Enter the exact amount in pounds and pence, for example 167.31

P60

You need a current P60 to use this option. Your P60 must have a ‘total for year’ figure of more than zero.

You will need to tell us the:

  • ‘total for year’ figure - this is in the ‘Pay and Income Tax details’ section. Use the figure in the column headed 'Pay'
  • ‘employee’s contributions’ figure - ​​this in the right-hand column. If you had more than one job, use the figure titled ‘In this employment’

Enter the exact amount in pounds and pence, for example 167.31

The details you give must be from a current claim. You will be asked to tell us:

  • the last 4 digits of the bank account your Tax Credits are paid into. You can find these details on your bank card, statement, online banking or a paying-in or cheque book
  • the amount of a recent Tax Credit payment that has been made to you. Enter the exact amount, including pence. You may find it useful to refer to your bank account to get this information.

To be able to use this option, your passport must be:

  • issued in the UK
  • not expired or cancelled
  • not reported as lost or stolen

Make sure you enter the details exactly as they appear on your passport. You will need to tell us:

  • your passport number and the expiry date
  • your surname and given names as shown on your passport

The data we use to match is provided by HMPO, and you will be asked to give your consent to us sharing the details you enter with them before you start.

Self Assessment

You will need to give us details of the most recent payment you made towards your Self Assessment. You may find it useful to refer to your bank account to get this information. You will need to tell us the:

  • exact amount of the payment, including pence
  • date the payment left your bank account

It can take up to 5 working days for payments to show in our system. If you made your last payment less than 5 working days ago, try using the details of the previous payment.

Pensions and benefits

You may be asked to tell us about your pensions and benefits, if you have any.

You need to use details from your tax returns from the last 2 years. Enter the exact amounts, noting that these figures are whole pounds and do not include pence. Enter 0 (zero) if a pension or benefit does not apply to you.

Has this helped you?

Answering these questions will not affect your credit rating.

You need a UK address and enough credit history to use this option. The data we use to match is provided by TransUnion, and you will be asked to give your consent to us sharing the details you enter with them before you start.

You will be asked up to five multiple choice questions about information that is held on your credit record, for example:

  • When you took out a mobile phone contract and which company provided it
  • When you took out a personal loan, credit card or mortgage and what the outstanding balance is
  • When you last opened a bank account and which bank provided it
